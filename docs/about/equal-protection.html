The Case for Equal Protection

The Biblical Mandate

The foundation of abolition is the Imago Dei—the truth that every human being is created in the image of God (Genesis 1:27). Because of this, God commands: "You shall not murder" (Exodus 20:13).

God does not distinguish between the value of a born child and an unborn child. He commands equal justice:

"You shall have the same rule for the sojourner and for the native, for I am the LORD your God." - Leviticus 24:22

To punish the murder of a born person while permitting the murder of an unborn person is partiality, which God hates (James 2:9). Equal Protection is simply the application of God's standard of justice to our laws.

The Constitutional Mandate

The 14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution states:

"No State shall... deny to any person within its jurisdiction the equal protection of the laws."

Since 1973, the courts have engaged in a fiction that the pre-born are not "persons." But biology and common sense tell us otherwise. A human fetus is a human being.

States have a duty to interpose—to stand between the oppressor and the victim—and assert that in Georgia, the unborn are persons, and they will be protected equally.

"Open your mouth for the mute, for the rights of all who are destitute." - Proverbs 31:8
