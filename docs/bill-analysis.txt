// HB 441 - The Abolition of Abortion Act
// Introduced: Regular Session, 2024
// Status: In Committee
// ----------------------------------------

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the "Abolition of Abortion in [State] Act."

SECTION 2. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS.
The Legislature finds that:
(1) Human life begins at fertilization.
(2) The State has a compelling interest in protecting the lives of all human beings from the moment of conception.
(3) Current statutes provide immunity for certain classes of homicide that must be repealed to establish equal justice.

SECTION 3. DEFINITION OF PERSON.
Section 1.01 of the Penal Code is amended to read:
"Person" means a human being who is alive, including an unborn child at every stage of gestation from fertilization until birth.

SECTION 4. REPEAL OF EXCEPTIONS.
(a) All defenses to prosecution for the homicide of an unborn child that do not apply to the homicide of a born child are hereby repealed.
(b) No provision of this Act shall be construed to prohibit medical treatment intended to save the life of the mother where the death of the unborn child is an unintended result.

SECTION 5. JURISDICTION.
The Attorney General shall have concurrent jurisdiction to prosecute offenses under this chapter if the local district attorney fails or refuses to prosecute.
