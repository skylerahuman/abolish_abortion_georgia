The Historical Record

Trace the legal history from the judicial fiat of 1973 to the legislative battles of today.

1973

Roe v. Wade

Jan 1973

The Supreme Court invents a 'right to privacy' that encompasses the right to kill a child. This unconstitutional decree stripped personhood from the unborn, leading to decades of bloodshed.

1973

Doe v. Bolton

Jan 1973

Released alongside Roe, this Georgia case defined 'health of the mother' so broadly (including familial, financial, and psychological factors) that it effectively allowed abortion on demand through all nine months.

1992

Planned Parenthood v. Casey

Jun 1992

The Court upheld the 'core holding' of Roe. While it allowed states to enact some regulations (waiting periods), it established the 'Undue Burden' standard, ensuring abortion remained protected.

2003

Partial-Birth Abortion Ban

Nov 2003

A pro-life regulatory victory that banned a specific, gruesome method of killing. However, abolitionists note that it simply directed abortionists to use other methods (like dismemberment) to achieve the same result legally.

2011

Rise of the Abolition Movement

Various 2011

Small groups of Christians began rejecting the 'Pro-Life Industry' strategy of incremental regulation. They began demanding immediate abolition and equal protection, planting the seeds for bills like HB 441.

Gospel Hope
2019

Georgia's 'Heartbeat' Bill (HB 481)

May 2019

Georgia bans abortion after a detectable heartbeat. While a step forward in saving some lives, it inherently denies personhood to those younger than ~6 weeks, leaving them without protection.

2022

Dobbs v. Jackson

Jun 2022

Roe v. Wade is overturned. The Constitution is correctly interpreted as not conferring a right to abortion. However, the Court failed to declare the unborn as persons, merely returning the issue to the states.

2023

The Georgia Equal Protection Act (HB 496)

Feb 2023

A precursor to HB 441, this bill sought to defy judicial tyranny and establish justice. It faced stiff opposition not just from Democrats, but from establishment Republican leadership.

Gospel Hope
2024

HB 441 on the Floor

Current 2024

The current fight. HB 441 aims to remove the exceptions in Georgia code, treating the pre-born with the exact same legal status as the born. It is the plea for consistency and justice.
